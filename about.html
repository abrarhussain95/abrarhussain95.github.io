About Me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Epidemiology at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. My research focuses on tick-borne diseases, Bayesian spatial modeling, and geospatial epidemiology to better understand disease risk and improve surveillance systems.

My work integrates quantitative modeling, spatial analysis, and public health data to address challenges such as underreporting and uncertainty in infectious disease systems.

I have experience working on tick-borne diseases in both Illinois and Pakistan, with a focus on ecological modeling and risk prediction.
